Andar fired bolts from his hand in a blinding wave of destruction.  Whereupon the lightning touched, the resultant infernoes did not merely light- they erupted. Each rock and tree, every leaf, every blade of grass bellowed out as if straining heretofore against some unseen bond.  Straining to keep their latent power, sealed across the ages, from undoing creation itself.  But now: free. Finally relieved of their burden by the lightest touch of Andar's power.

The battleworn soldiers barely flinched at their doom.  For months, their eventual deaths had been more real to them than the prospects of waking the next morning at all.  The lighting scattered across the ranks, and the bodies burst into a red, smothering mist.
